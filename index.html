Conviction, not certainty. STRONG has been right 68.8% of the time — which means it has been wrong nearly a third of the time.

Calibrated against our own graded results, not a raw model output. Capped at 75%, because we have never actually observed a bucket win more often than that.
FAIR → what the price should be
P(win) written as odds. FAIR −231 while you are offered −190 means you are buying below fair value. Offered −300, you are overpaying — even if the pick is sound.

The most important badge on the board. It appears only when P(win) beats the break-even of the price on offer.

4 · How to actually use this
1. Shop the price. Every card names the book with the best number we found. On a typical slate, FanDuel holds the best price on fewer than one rung in three. The same bet at two books is often the difference between +EV and −EV.

2. Weight the CLEARS. If you want the version of this board with the strongest historical record, lean on the green badge.

3. Respect the tier sizing. STRONG is 2u for a reason. Flat-betting every signal throws away the model’s best information — its ranking.

4. Do not chase the long alternate lines. They look like value and the model is least reliable exactly there. See section 7.
5 · What the model actually does
No black box — and no formula either. Here is the honest shape of it.
Recent form, adjusted for who he faced
The last ten starts, each re-scaled for that opponent’s strikeout rate. Seven Ks against a contact team is a better start than seven against a whiffing one, and the model treats it that way.
Stuff and command
Whiff rate, putaway rate, first-pitch-strike rate — each measured against league average. A pitcher who misses bats and works ahead turns more plate appearances into strikeouts.
Tonight’s specific lineup
Not the team’s season average — the nine batters actually posted, and how each of them fares against this pitcher’s particular pitch mix.
Volatility
Some arms are metronomes; some swing from 3 Ks to 11. The more erratic the arm, the less the model trusts recent form and the more it leans on the underlying rate.

8 · Where it fails
Volatile arms against contact lineups. The model over-projects here, consistently. Cards carrying σ-CUT have already been trimmed for it, and it remains the weakest matchup on the board.

The long alternate rungs. The further above the main line you climb, the more the model disagrees with the market — and the market is usually right. Those rungs are the model’s worst-calibrated region. They will look like value. Be sceptical.

Paying juice. Worth repeating, because it costs the most: this board wins 58.8% of its bets and still loses money at bad prices. The pick is only half the bet.
